ANTS

Latch onto me,
Grapple my waist and remind me
Of what it took, to get to this place
How the grind of my bones felt

On this pavement I followed so religiously
But I was never religious, you see
Bound to this conscious tumour
So wicked and unforgiving

Watch me claw at the faucets
Beg for my life back
Forgive me, forget me
Is this meant to be winning?

Why does my body crawl with fire
Ants dancing through my bloodstream
Dispersing into fingertips gone numb
Did you think I was done? This is just the beginning

Wouldn’t have guessed I’d turn out like this
So don’t question my screaming
This language so foreign, hissing on my tongue
Yet I sit, unbelievably still in the chaos

Thinking this way is a customary sin
Reality gone blurry to the craving
Weathered with time I’ve stopped squinting
Scraping at my insides for an answer

Pouring my insecurities, past tendencies
Spitting from my mouth, gagging common sense
Scouring my taste buds into submission
Preparation for a road to death’s bed

Watching the ants roll by in waves
Their slick, oil like demeanor a welcomed escape
Rolling back my neck, lull of an eye
Let them consume me, use me

Am I really that insane

or is it the ants talking?
